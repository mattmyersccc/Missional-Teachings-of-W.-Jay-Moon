Dr. W. Jay Moon brings a unique blend of academic rigor, missionary experience, and entrepreneurial thinking to the fields of missiology and church planting. Having spent 13 years in Ghana, West Africa, he gained deep insights into oral cultures and the importance of contextualizing the Gospel. His work is characterized by a passion for finding innovative and effective ways to make disciples and establish faith communities in diverse, often overlooked, settings.

This interactive resource provides an overview of his key contributions, focusing on his models for church planting, his approach to discipleship through storytelling, and his framework for evangelism across different cultural worldviews.

Key Publications

Dive deeper into Dr. Moon's research and practical insights through his influential books.

Effective Intercultural Evangelism

Co-authored with W. Bud Simon, this book unpacks how to share the Gospel across the four major worldviews (Guilt/Justice, Shame/Honor, Fear/Power, Indifference/Belonging).

Intercultural Discipleship

Explores disciple-making in multiple cultures, emphasizing contextual approaches that are biblically faithful and culturally relevant, especially for oral learners.

Entrepreneurial Church Planting

Provides innovative strategies for engaging business and the marketplace as a mission field for planting new churches.

A Missional Approach to the Marketplace

Invites Christians to view their work and the marketplace as integral to their missional calling, reaching people where they spend most of their time.

African Proverbs Reveal Christianity

Draws from his time in Ghana to show how indigenous wisdom, like proverbs, can be a bridge for contextualizing and understanding Christian theology.
